# 慈航 (Cí Háng) — Compassionate Passage

## A Design Philosophy for Healing Across Water

---

### I. The Movement

**慈航** emerges from the act of crossing — a hospital's compassion traveling across the Taiwan Strait to an island fortress turned peaceful haven. The visual language is built on the tension between two worlds: the ancient and the modern, the military and the merciful, the stone walls of Kinmen's *gǔcuò* (古厝) and the sterile precision of medical care. This philosophy treats the poster as a votive object — something painstakingly wrought, meant to be held and studied, not glanced at. Every element is the product of deep expertise, labored over as if the act of design itself were an act of care.

### II. Space and Form

Space breathes here through asymmetric geometry — a dominant vertical blue field anchors authority and trust, while horizontal gold bands cut across like tidal currents, suggesting both the Taiwan Strait and the pulse of a healthy heart. Kinmen's *swallowtail rooflines* appear as a ghosted silhouette at the horizon line — barely visible, like memory embedded in landscape. Forms are architectural: strong, load-bearing, permanent. Nothing floats without purpose.

### III. Color and Material

The palette is a meditation on depth: **Navy #1B3A6B** — the color of deep sea and institutional trust — holds the composition like the walls of a fort. **Gold #F5A623** — the color of sorghum under afternoon sun, of brass incense urns, of lamp-lit operating theaters — punctuates with warmth and urgency. White is not empty space but breath: oxygen, sterile cloth, morning mist over the strait. These three colors work as a system — never decorative, always structural.

### IV. Scale, Rhythm, and Typographic Hierarchy

Typography is meticulous to the point of obsession. The headline lives at a scale that commands — Chinese characters rendered at maximum weight, their strokes as deliberate as calligraphic brushwork yet rooted in grotesque precision. Beneath it, information cascades through a carefully calibrated hierarchy: medium weight for dates and services, light weight for locations and fine print. The rhythm alternates between mass and restraint, loud and whispered. No two text levels share the same visual weight. This is the product of painstaking typographic craft.

### V. Composition and Visual Hierarchy

The composition follows a modified golden section: the viewer's eye enters at the bold headline, moves downward through color-coded date blocks, then settles at the logo anchor. Service icons are arranged as a systematic grid — clinical, orderly, reassuring. The overall structure is that of a *triptych*: sky (institution), body (event), earth (logistics). Information is encoded spatially: what you need to know first lives highest, what grounds the piece lives lowest. This master-level execution ensures every element earns its position through visual logic, not decoration.

---

*This philosophy insists on one truth: that design made in service of healing must itself be an act of care — meticulous, unhurried, executed by someone at the absolute top of their craft.*
